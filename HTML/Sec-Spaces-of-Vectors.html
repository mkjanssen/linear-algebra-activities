Section 2.2 Oct. 1: Spaces of Vectors

Subsection 2.2.1 On Vector Spaces and Subspaces

Exploration 2.2.1.
Let \(\mathcal{P}\) be the set of polynomials of degree 3 or less. Explain why \(\mathcal{P}\) is a vector space, or explain why it is not.
Exploration 2.2.2.
Explain why the following are subspaces of \(\mathbb{R}^3\text{.}\)
  • Any line through \((0,0,0)\text{.}\)
  • The set of all \((x,y,z)\) satisfying \(3x-4y+z=0\text{.}\)
Exploration 2.2.3.
Consider the vector space \(\mathbb{R}^2\text{.}\) Explain why the following are not subspaces:
  • The unit circle.
  • The line \(x+y = 4\text{.}\)
  • The union of lines \(2x+3y = 0\) and \(x-y=0\text{.}\)
  • The first quadrant where \(x\geq 0\) and \(y\geq 0\text{.}\)
Exploration 2.2.4.
Let \(W\) be the set of continuous functions \(f\) in \(\mathcal{C}(\mathbb{R})\) which satisfy \(f''(x) = 0\text{.}\) Decide if \(W\) is a subspace of \(\mathcal{C}(\mathbb{R})\text{,}\) and explain your thinking.
