Exploration 7.1.2.

A discrete (linear) dynamical system \(x_{k+1} = A x_k\text{,}\) which describes the long-term behavior of a state vector \(x_k\text{,}\) is best understood via the eigenvalues and eigenvectors of \(A\text{,}\) as described in this example.

In the redwood forests of California, dusky-footed wood rats provide up to 80% of the diet for the spotted owl, the main predator of the wood rat. Let's build a linear model to describe the basic physical system of the owls and the rats.

Denote the owl and wood rat populations at time \(k\) by the vector \(x_k = \begin{bmatrix} O_k \\ R_k \end{bmatrix}\text{,}\) where \(k\) is the time in months, \(O_k\) the number of wols in the region studied, and \(R_k\) the numer of rats (measured in thousands). Suppose

\begin{align*} O_{k+1} &= (0.5) O_k + (0.4)R_k\\ R_{k+1} &= (-p) O_k + (1.1)R_k, \end{align*}

where \(p\) is a positive parameter to be specified.

  1. Ignoring the \(R_k\) term in the first equation, what does \((0.5) O_k\) mean? Answer similar questions for the other three terms on the right-hand sides of the equations.
  2. Assume \(p=0.104\text{.}\) Write a coefficient matrix \(A\) satisfying \(x_{k+1}= A x_k\text{.}\)
  3. Find the eigenvalues and associated eigenvectors of \(A\text{.}\)
  4. Diagonalize \(A\text{,}\) if possible.
  5. Let \(v_1, v_2\) be the eigenvectors of \(A\text{.}\) Why can any initial state \(x_0\) be written as a linear combination \(x_0 = c_1 v_1 +c_2 v_2\text{?}\)
  6. Now explain why \(x_k = c_1 A^k v_1 + c_2 A^k v_2\) for any \(k > 0\text{.}\) Use whatever numbers you've found in place of the letters.
  7. As \(k\to \infty\text{,}\) what does \(x_{k+1}\) approach (in terms of \(x_k\))?
